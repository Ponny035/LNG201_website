Literature Review (Leo)



The first following article is from King Abdul Aziz University
"The effects of YouTube in multimedia instruction for Vocabulary Learning"
Retrieved from: https://doi.org/10.5539/elt.v11n2p72


This first article is an analysis that has been made by the Canadian center of science and education. The purpose of this study was to compare the evolution of vocabulary comprehension and retention of 4 classes of 25 students. They made an evaluation of the level of English vocabulary and retention at the beginning of the study. They were charged to watch videos in English on YouTube every day during 2 weeks. The videos they were charged to watch were not videos about vocabulary or grammar. That was just funny videos that they enjoyed to watch. After two weeks they made the same evaluation about English vocabulary and retention. All of the students have significantly increased their comprehension about the vocabulary. But surprisingly most of them didn’t increase their level about talking in English. The center conclude that this study was really interesting because that was a proof that even if you are not studying you can improve your capabilities in English by using YouTube. They also conclude that this study could be applied to other fields to see if YouTube can also be useful for this other fields and maybe become a new tool in teaching.



The second article is from Middle School Journal
"Digital storytelling: A tool for teaching and learning in the YouTube generation"
Retrieved from: https://eric.ed.gov/?id=EJ934075


This second article was made by Oliver Dreon, Richar M. Kerper and Jon Landis, it is extracted from the middle school journal. This article is an analysis about how most of the humans are reacting when they see videos on YouTube. There is millions and millions of videos on YouTube, there is more video reference in YouTube than in any other platforms or social media. We estimate that in 2018, more than 80% of the active population was connected on YouTube each month. Some of the videos that we can find on YouTube have more than 6 billions of view. So we can say that most of the active humans like to watch videos. More than that, we can assume that the current generation and even more the young generation are formatted to watch videos. So they made an interesting experiment to see the reaction of people. In a public place they put 2 big rooms. In this rooms there were two things, in one of them there was a graduated teacher that was selected for his brilliant and attractive courses about quantum physics. In the other rooms they put a big screen with a course about the same subject as the professor but this time the course was chosen for his monotony. They placed some cameras to count the number of people in each room during the day. At the end of the day, there was more people that have seen the movie course than the real course. It’s an interesting study because we can see that even if a course is boring, just because it’s a course on a screen most of the people will prefer the movie course as the real course. It’s a proof that our generation is formatted to watch videos and also that if we can control the subject that people are watching, they can be taught without being aware of it.



The third following article is from Princess Noura Bint Abdulrahman University
"The impact of using YouTube in EFL classroom on enhancing EFL students’ content learning"
Retrieved from:https://eric.ed.gov/?id=EJ1061416


This last article is an analysis of a study that has been made by Huda Omar Alwehaibi on a group of 95 students in an elementary school. The purpose of this study was to see if watching videos on YouTube can improve the English level of students that are not English native. There were 2 groups of student to better show the difference of the two way of teaching. One group will receive a traditional teaching and the other will receive a teaching more based on some videos on YouTube but also with a part of traditional teaching. This study lasted 6 weeks and the subjects were supposed to watch around 2 hours of videos per week. They also receive a teaching based on some power-point presentations. Another time the group that received the teaching more based on some YouTube videos and power-point presentation improve more his skills in English than the other. The teacher conclude that the fact of using some YouTube videos in addition of a traditional teaching could be an important teaching resource and he recommended to other teacher to use his method.

Interview Case Studies (Pon)

Our group interviewed three people which consisting of one Ph.D. student and two alumni. As a group, we created four open-ended questions about “YouTube as an online learning platform” We wanted to know how much YouTube's video effect to them and their perspectives about online learning platform. All interviews were arranged well in advance and flexibility of interview times was essential. Each interview was recorded to be used on our group’s website. We asked permission from all interviewees if that was acceptable to them. They all answered positively.


This are four open-ended questions that we used.
  1. Why do you an online learning platform as your learning resource?
  2. Do you think that videos in YouTube are realiable or not. Why?
  3. Do you think that Youtube can be an online learning platform. Why?
  4. What is your opinion if I say that "YouTube is more effective than class room in term of learning"?
